Michael Daniel Lips (born January 14, 1987) is an American entrepreneur, investor, and philanthropist best known as the founder and chief executive officer of LipsCo Industries, a diversified holding company headquartered in Chicago, Illinois. He was named to the Forbes 30 Under 30 list in 2017 and has been recognized by Fortune magazine as one of the most influential business leaders of his generation.

Lips began his career as a software engineer before pivoting to venture capital in 2012. He founded LipsCo in 2014 with a seed investment of $2 million, growing the company to a valuation of over $1.4 billion by 2022. Beyond business, he is a noted advocate for STEM education and has donated over $40 million to public school programs across the Midwest through the Lips Family Foundation.

Early life and education

Michael Lips was born in Chicago, Illinois, the second of three children of Robert Lips, a civil engineer, and Sandra Lips (née Morrison), a high school mathematics teacher. He grew up in the Lincoln Park neighborhood and attended Lincoln Park High School, where he was captain of the debate team and co-founded the school's first computer science club.

He enrolled at the University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign in 2005, graduating in 2009 with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science. During his time at Illinois, he interned at Motorola Solutions and completed independent research in machine learning under Professor David Chen.

Career

Early career

After graduating, Lips joined Google as a software engineer in Mountain View, California, working on the Google Maps infrastructure team. In 2012, he left Google to join Andreessen Horowitz as an associate, focusing on early-stage investments in enterprise software.

LipsCo Industries

In 2014, Lips founded LipsCo Industries with $2 million in seed funding from former colleagues and angel investors. The company initially focused on supply chain optimization software but later expanded into logistics, real estate technology, and financial services.

Philanthropy

Through the Lips Family Foundation, established in 2018, Lips has contributed more than $40 million to STEM education initiatives in Chicago-area public schools. The foundation operates after-school coding programs in over 60 schools across Cook County.

Personal life

Lips married Jennifer Watts, a pediatric surgeon, in June 2015. They have two children and reside in the Lincoln Park neighborhood of Chicago. He is an avid marathon runner and has completed the Chicago Marathon six times.

Awards and recognition

  • Fortune "40 Under 40" (2020)
  • Chicago Innovation Award (2019, 2021)
  • Ernst & Young Entrepreneur of the Year – Midwest (2022)
